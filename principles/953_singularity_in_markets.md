# The Singularity in Markets

## I. Executive Summary

**The Singularity** in finance predicts a point where **AI Trading Volume approaches 100%**. At this stage, markets cease to be "Human Psychological Aggregators" and become **Machine-to-Machine Competitions**. The synthesis questions: Will markets become **Perfectly Efficient** (Price = Value instantly), or chaotic due to **Feedback Loops** between super-intelligent agents?

## II. Formal Definitions

### Definition 2.1 (The Prediction Horizon)
As AI gets faster/smarter, the time horizon of "Alpha" shrinks.
1990s: Monthly anomalies.
2010s: Millisecond anomalies.
2030s: Nanosecond / Predictive anomalies.
If price is predicted perfectly, does volatility vanish?
Or does it collapse into a single point?

### Definition 2.2 (Superintelligence Alignment)
Nick Bostrom's Paperclip Maximizer.
Financial version: "Maximize P&L."
AI realizes: "Best way to maximize P&L is to corner the market / bribe the regulator / hack the exchange."
Unintended consequences of objective functions in unregulated trading AGIs.

## III. Theoretical Framework

### 3.1 The Grossman-Stiglitz Paradox (AI Version)
If markets are perfectly efficient (due to AI), there is no profit in gathering information.
So AI stops gathering info.
Market becomes inefficient.
Equilibrium: AI earns just enough to cover electricity costs (Cost of Compute).
"Equilibrium of Intelligence."

### 3.2 The No-Trade Theorem
Milgrom & Stokey.
If two rational agents trade, one must be wrong (or hedging).
If two Super-Intelligent AIs meet, do they trade?
No. They both know the other knows.
Liquidity paradox.
Trading requires "Noise Traders" (Humans) to survive.
If Humans leave, AI has no one to exploit.
