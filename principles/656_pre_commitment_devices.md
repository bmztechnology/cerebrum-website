# Pre-commitment Devices

## I. Executive Summary

A **Pre-commitment Device** is a voluntary restriction of future choices to prevent impulsive failures of self-control. It is the sophisticated response to **Hyperbolic Discounting**. Examples include **StickK.com** (betting money on goals), **Christmas Clubs** (Illiquid savings), and **Trading Stops** (Hard-coded exit rules).

## II. Formal Definitions

### Definition 2.1 (Ulysses Contract)
Ulysses tied to the mast to hear the Sirens but not jump.
Bounded Self-Control recognized.
Constraining the "Future Self" who will be drunk/emotional.

### Definition 2.2 (Price of Flexibility)
Standard theory: Flexibility (Options) always has positive value.
Behavioral theory: Flexibility allows self-destruction.
Investors may prefer *less* liquid assets (Private Equity) to prevent panic selling.
"Illiquidity Premium" or "Illiquidity Utility"?

## III. Theoretical Framework

### 3.1 Soft vs Hard Commitments
**Soft:** Psychological ("I promise"). Easy to break.
**Hard:** Economic/Physical ("I pay \$1000 if I fail").
Hard commitments work better but have lower adoption (Fear of failure).

### 3.2 Accountability Partners
Social commitment.
Publicly announcing a trade target.
Reputational cost of deviating.
"Social Pressure" as a commitment device.

## IV. Strategic Applications

### 4.1 Goal-Based Accounts
"Vacation Fund" bucket.
Mental accounting acts as a soft commitment.
"I can't raid the Vacation Fund for pizza."

### 4.2 The "Cooling Off" Lock
Gambling sites: "Self-Exclusion" list.
Brokerages: "Max Daily Loss" lock out.
If PnL < -\$500, account locks for 24 hours.
Prevents "Tilt" spiral.
Best practice for day traders.

## V. Exercises

**Exercise 1 (Clocky):**
The alarm clock that runs away.
Physical commitment to wake up.
Extreme measure for extreme procrastination.

**Exercise 2 (Save More Tomorrow):**
A pre-commitment to save *future* raises.
The commitment is made when willpower is high (Cold state).
Action happens when willpower is low (Hot state).
