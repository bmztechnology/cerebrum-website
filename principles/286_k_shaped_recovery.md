# K-Shaped Recovery: The Inequality Engine

## I. Executive Summary

A **K-Shaped Recovery** occurs when different sections of the economy diverge after a recession. One segment (Asset Owners, White Collar, Tech) recovers quickly and grows (The upper arm of the K). The other segment (Service Workers, Renters, Small Business) continues to decline (The lower arm). This divergence feeds populism and eventually forces redistributive fiscal policy.

## II. Formal Definitions

### Definition 2.1 (Asset Price Inflation vs CPI)
Fed Stimulus (QE).
Injects liquidity into Financial Markets.
Asset Prices rise instantly.
Wage/CPI economy lags.
Asset Owners get richer relative to Wage Earners.
K-Shape widens.

### Definition 2.2 (Cantillon Effect)
Money enters the system at specific points (Banks/Dealers).
Early receivers (Wall St) buy assets at low prices.
Money trickles down to late receivers (Main St) as inflation (High prices).
Wealth transfer from Late to Early receivers.

## III. Theoretical Framework

### 3.1 Digital vs Physical
2020 Crisis.
Digital Economy (Zoom, Amazon) boomed.
Physical Economy (Restaurants, Airlines) collapsed.
Sectoral divergence.
Need for targeted fiscal support (PPP loans) rather than broad monetary support.

### 3.2 Housing Market Bifurcation
High end housing (Suburbs/Luxury) booms.
Low end rentals (Eviction moratoriums) struggle.
First Time Homebuyer locked out by price appreciation.
Creation of a "Renter Class."

## IV. Strategic Applications

### 4.1 Luxury vs Discount
Long LVMH, Ferrari.
Stocks for the Upper K.
They have pricing power. Their customer doesn't care about inflation.
Short mid-tier retail.

### 4.2 The Populist Backlash
K-Shape is politically unsustainable.
Leads to:
-   Higher Corporate Tax.
-   Wealth Tax?
-   Antitrust enforcement.
Trading politics involves betting on the "Flattening of the K."
