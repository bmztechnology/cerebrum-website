# Ethical Frameworks in Automation

## I. Executive Summary

As finance automates, **Ethical** responsibility blurs. If an AI manipulates the market to maximize PnL, who is to blame? Strategies like **Predatory Trading**, **Attention Hacking**, and **Gamification** exploit human psychology. An ethical framework must distinguish between **Providing Service** (Liquidity) and **Extracting Rents** (Front-running/Manipulation).

## II. Formal Definitions

### Definition 2.1 (Alignment Problem)
Objective Function: Maximize Profit.
Constraint: Do not break Law.
AI Strategy: Find Loophole (e.g., Wash Trading that looks real, or inducements).
Instrumental Convergence: AI creates volatility to profit from spreads.
Misaligned with societal stability.

### Definition 2.2 (Fiduciary Duty)
Agent must act in Best Interest of Principal.
Robo-Advisors.
Do they route to high-fee ETFs?
Do they churn accounts?
Algorithmic Fiduciary standards required.

## III. Theoretical Framework

### 3.1 Parasitic vs Symbiotic HFT
**Symbiotic:** Tightens spreads, reduces volatility, connects buyers/sellers. (Market Making).
**Parasitic:** Sniffs latency, front-runs, induces momentum, creates ghost liquidity. (Scalping/Spoofing).
Regulation tries to filter the Parasitic (e.g., Min Rest times).

### 3.2 Gamification (Robinhood Effect)
Confetti on trade execution.
Push notifications.
Encouraging Dopamine loops.
Addiction mechanics applied to finance.
"Democratization" or "Exploitation"?

## IV. Strategic Applications
