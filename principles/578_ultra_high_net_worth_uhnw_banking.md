# Ultra High Net Worth UHNW Banking

## I. Executive Summary

**UHNW Banking** services clients with investable assets typically exceeding \$30M. Unlike retail or mass-affluent sectors, the value proposition is not just investment management but **Balance Sheet Management** (Lending against art/jets), **Concierge Services**, and access to **Private Deal Flow**. Banks effectively act as the **Prime Broker** for the individual.

## II. Formal Definitions

### Definition 2.1 (Lombard Lending)
Secured lending against liquid portfolio.
LTV (Loan to Value): 50-70% on Equities, 90% on Treasuries.
Rate: SOFR + Spread (typical spread < 100bps for UHNW).
Allows client to buy a yacht without selling stocks (avoiding Tax Event).

### Definition 2.2 (Open Architecture)
Historically, banks sold proprietary products.
UHNW clients demand "Best of Breed" external managers.
Bank acts as a platform/aggregator.
Revenue shifts from Management Fees to Custody/Advisory Fees.

## III. Theoretical Framework

### 3.1 The Trusted Advisor
Relationship Manager (RM) is the key asset.
High turnover of RMs leads to client churn.
Banks try to institutionalize the relationship (Team approach).
"Share of Wallet."

### 3.2 Complex Assets
Art, Wine, Classic Cars.
Bank provides valuations and storage solutions.
Liquidity facilities against illiquid passion assets.
Diversification argument? Or consumption disguised as investment?

## IV. Strategic Applications

### 4.1 Cross Border Solutions
Client lives in London, family in Delaware, business in Hong Kong.
Multi-jurisdictional tax reporting.
Currency hedging.
"Booking Centers" (Jersey, Switzerland, Singapore).

### 4.2 Capital Markets Access
Direct IPO allocation.
Pre-IPO placements.
Structured Notes (Customized derivatives).
Selling Volatility via Accumulators.

## V. Exercises

**Exercise 1 (The Structured Note):**
Bank issues note: "If S&P 500 > -20%, pay 8% coupon. If < -20%, loss of principal."
Selling a Put Option.
Client yields 8%. Bank hedges the Put.
Risks: Lehman Brothers (Counterparty risk of issuer).

**Exercise 2 (Net Interest Margin):**
Banks make more money lending to UHNW than managing assets.
The "Stickiness" of a loan relationship.
