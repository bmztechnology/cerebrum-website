# The Physics of Wall Street

## I. Executive Summary

Finance has long imported models from **Physics** (Brownian Motion, Heat Equation). This chapter synthesizes the **Econophysics** revolution. It argues that while Physics provides powerful tools (Calculus, Probability), it often commits the **reification fallacy**: mistaking the *Model* for the *Territory*. Markets are not particles; they are people.

## II. Formal Definitions

### Definition 2.1 (The Random Walk - Brownian Motion)
Einstein (1905) / Bachelier (1900).
Particle moves randomly.
Market price moves randomly.
Basis of Black-Scholes.
Assumption: Independence of events.
Reality: Memory exists (Hurst Exponent). Markets trend.

### Definition 2.2 (Power Laws - Pareto)
Physics: Height of humans is Normal Distribution (Gaussian).
Physics: Earthquake magnitude is Power Law (Pareto).
Finance: Price returns are Power Law.
The "3 Sigma" event happens every 5 years, not every 10,000 years.
Gaussian models underestimate Risk by orders of magnitude.

## III. Theoretical Framework

### 3.1 Gauge Invariance
Symmetry in physics.
Does the "Money Gauge" matter?
If I pay in USD or EUR, value is same.
But if the Gauge (Money) is expanding (Inflation), the grid wraps.
We must normalize for the distortion of the measuring stick.

### 3.2 Turbulence
Navier-Stokes equations (Fluid Dynamics).
Modeling money flow as a fluid.
Laminar Flow (Bull Market).
Turbulent Flow (Crash).
Reynolds Number (Leverage).
High Leverage $\implies$ Onset of Turbulence.

## IV. Strategic Applications

### 4.1 The renormalization Group
Analyzing systems at different scales.
Fractals.
Price patterns look the same on 1-min chart and 1-year chart.
Scale Invariance.
Allows applying the same logic (Trend Following) to all timeframes.

### 4.2 Maximum Entropy
Jaynes.
The probability distribution that best represents the current state of knowledge is the one with largest entropy.
Used in Portfolio Optimization (Risk Parity).
avoiding "False Certainty."
