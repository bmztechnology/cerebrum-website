# IMF Program Mechanics: Conditionality

## I. Executive Summary

The **International Monetary Fund (IMF)** acts as the global **Lender of Last Resort**. When a country faces a Balance of Payments crisis, the IMF provides capital (SDRs) in exchange for policy reforms (**Conditionality**). This typically involves **Austerity** (Fiscal consolidation) and Structural Reforms aimed at restoring **Debt Sustainability**.

## II. Formal Definitions

### Definition 2.1 (DSA)
**Debt Sustainability Analysis (DSA).**
Project debt trajectory over 5-10 years.
Variables: Growth, Primary Balance, Interest Rates.
Conclusion:
-   **Sustainable:** Liquidity support (Stand-By Arrangement).
-   **Unsustainable:** Requires Restructuring (Haircut) *before* IMF lends.

### Definition 2.2 (Tranche Releases)
Money not given upfront.
Quarterly reviews.
"Did you raise VAT? Did you devalue currency?"
If Yes $\implies$ Release next tranche.
If No $\implies$ Program "Off Track." Waiver needed.

## III. Theoretical Framework

### 3.1 The Catalyst Effect
IMF money is small relative to needs.
But IMF "Seal of Approval" unlocks:
1.  Paris Club relief.
2.  Private Sector capital flows (Confidence).
3.  Multilateral Development Bank (World Bank) loans.

### 3.2 Moral Hazard
If IMF always bails out, Countries borrow recklessly and Lenders lend recklessly.
"Exceptional Access Framework" limits bailouts?
Often overridden for "Systemic" countries (Argentina, Greece).

## IV. Strategic Applications

### 4.1 Prior Actions
Painful reforms required *before* the Board approves the loan.
Devaluation of exchange rate.
Removal of fuel subsidies.
Politically explosive ($ \to $ Riots).

### 4.2 Trading the Program
Buy bonds on rumors of IMF deal.
Sell on implementation risks.
"Buy the rumor, sell the fact."
Asset prices rally massively on Program Approval (Anchor of stability).
