# Sovereign Wealth Funds SWF

## I. Executive Summary

**Sovereign Wealth Funds (SWFs)** are state-owned investment vehicles managing trillions in surplus (Oil, Trade). They shift capital from **Hoarding** (Reserves in T-Bills) to **Investing** (Equity/Real Estate). The synthesis focuses on **Geopolitical Capital**â€”how nations use SWFs (Norway, China, Saudi) to exert **Soft Power** and secure **Strategic Assets** (Tech, Infrastructure).

## II. Formal Definitions

### Definition 2.1 (Stabilization vs Savings)
**Stabilization Fund:** Cushions commodity shocks (Russia). Liquid assets. If Oil drops, drawdown fund to balance budget.
**Savings Fund:** Intergenerational wealth transfer (Norway). Long-term assets. "Dutch Disease" prevention (recycling Forex capability outside the domestic economy).

### Definition 2.2 (State Capitalism)
The blurring of Public and Private.
Saudi PIF investing in Uber, Lucid, SoftBank.
State directing innovation via venture capital.
"Leviathan as LP."
Conflict: Political goals (Vision 2030) vs Financial Returns.

## III. Theoretical Framework

### 3.1 The Dutch Disease
Large resource discovery (Oil) $\implies$ Currency appreciates $\implies$ Manufacturing dies.
Solution: Sterilize the inflows.
Park the dollars abroad in an SWF.
Keep domestic currency stable.
SWF is a macroeconomic tool, not just a hedge fund.

### 3.2 The Santiago Principles
Voluntary code of conduct for SWFs.
Transparency, Independence, Governance.
Designed to calm Western fears about "Foreign Takeovers."
Norway (NBIM) is the Gold Standard (Transparent).
Others (GIC, ADIA) are opaque.

## IV. Strategic Applications

### 4.1 Strategic Development (Domestic)
Mubadala / PIF.
Invest abroad to bring tech home.
"We give you \$1B, you open a factory in Riyadh."
Tech transfer via capital allocation.
Reversing the colonial flow.

### 4.2 The Universal Owner
Norway owns 1.5% of *every* listed company in the world.
Cannot divest risk (too big).
Must manage "Beta" (Global Growth).
Becomes an ESG enforcer.
"If the world burns, we lose."
Universal Ownership theory $\implies$ Strong focus on externalities (Climate).
