# Investor Education Programs

## I. Executive Summary

**Investor Education** initiatives are mandated by regulators and offered by firms, but their efficacy is debated. Effective programs move beyond "Information Dump" to **Skill Building** and **Debiasing**. Content is evolving from textbooks to **Simulators**, **Gamified Learning**, and **Community-Based Learning**. The goal is creating **Informed Consent** and resilience against fraud.

## II. Formal Definitions

### Definition 2.1 (The Teachable Moment)
Life events: Marriage, First Child, Divorce, Inheritance.
Receptivity to education peaks here.
Marketing education to these triggers.

### Definition 2.2 (Debiasing Training)
Can you teach people to not be biased?
Video games (Clemen).
"Oh no, you fell for Sunk Cost Fallacy!"
Feedback training reduces bias magnitude by 20-30%.
Hard, but possible.

## III. Theoretical Framework

### 3.1 Experiential Learning
Kolb's Cycle.
Concrete Experience (Trading Sim) $\to$ Observation $\to$ Conceptualization $\to$ Experimentation.
Paper Trading accounts (TD Ameritrade thinkorswim).
Risk: "Paper Money" breeds bad habits (No fear).

### 3.2 Social Learning Theory
Bandura.
Learning by observing others.
"Investment Clubs."
Online communities (Fintwit).
Danger: If the models are bad (Meme stock pumpers), learning is negative (Bad habits transmitted).
