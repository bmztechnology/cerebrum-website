# IMF Program Mechanics: The Lender of Last Resort

## I. Executive Summary

The **International Monetary Fund (IMF)** provides emergency loans to countries facing Balance of Payments crises. In exchange, the country must agree to strict **Conditionality** (Structural Adjustment Programs): Austerity, Privatization, and Currency Devaluation. The goal is to restore solvency and market access, but the social cost is often severe, leading to the stigma of "losing sovereignty."

## II. Formal Definitions

### Definition 2.1 (SBA - Stand-By Arrangement)
The standard loan. Duration 12-24 months.
Funds released in "Tranches."
Review: Every 3 months, IMF team checks if targets (Deficit, Reserves, Inflation) are met.
Miss target $\implies$ Next tranche withheld.

### Definition 2.2 (Debt Sustainability Analysis - DSA)
The Excel sheet that decides fate.
IMF models the debt path.
If Debt is deemed "Unsustainable," IMF *cannot* lend (by statute).
Country MUST default/restructure private debt first to get IMF money.
"Bailing in" private creditors.

## III. Theoretical Framework

### 3.1 Moral Hazard
If IMF always bails out countries, investors lend recklessly.
"The IMF Put."
To counter this, IMF loans are Senior (Preferred Creditor Status).
If country defaults, IMF gets paid first. Bondholders get zero.

### 3.2 The Washington Consensus
Formula:
1.  Fiscal Consolidation (Cut spending).
2.  Tight Monetary Policy (Hike rates).
3.  Currency Float (Devalue).
Critique: Pro-cyclical. Worsens the recession in the short run to fix the balance sheet in the long run.

## IV. Strategic Applications

### 4.1 The "IMF Rally"
When a country signs an IMF deal:
Bonds Rally (Default risk drops).
Currency Stabilizes (Reserves replenished).
Stocks Rally? Maybe, depending on Austerity impact.
Trade: Buy Sovereign Bonds on the rumor of IMF deal.

### 4.2 Program Failure
Argentina (2018). Largest IMF loan in history ($57B).
Failed. Capital flight continued.
Program collapsed.
"IMF Fatigue": Populations revolt against austerity.
Result: Crash is worse than before.
