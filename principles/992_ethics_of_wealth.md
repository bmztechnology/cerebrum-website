# The Ethics of Wealth

## I. Executive Summary

Wealth is power. Power requires **Responsibility**. The synthesis explores the moral dimension of accumulation. Is Wealth a **Sign of Virtue** (Protestant Ethic) or **Theft** (Marx)? The Master Trader understands that money is neutral energy, but the *pursuit* reveals character. **Stewarship** replaces ownership as the final evolutionary stage of the wealthy mind.

## II. Formal Definitions

### Definition 2.1 (The Gospel of Wealth - Carnegie)
"The man who dies thus rich dies disgraced."
Three modes of wealth disposal:
1. Leave to family (Spoils them).
2. Bequeath to public (State wastes it).
3. Administer during life (True philanthropy).
The duty of the wealthy is to solve problems the State cannot.

### Definition 2.2 (Effective Altruism - Singer)
Rational Charity.
\$1000 can save a life (Malaria net).
Or buy a suit.
Moral obligation to maximize impact.
Earn to Give.
High Frequency Trading to fund curing cancer?
The utilitarian calculus.

## III. Theoretical Framework

### 3.1 Money as Karmic Energy
Vedic view. Artha (Wealth) is one of the 4 goals of life.
But it must serve Dharma (Duty).
If wealth is gained via Adharma (Fraud/Harm), it brings destruction.
Clean Capital vs Dirty Capital.
ESG as a modern expression of Karma?

### 3.2 Biblical/Traditional Constraints
Usury bans. Sharia Law.
Warning against the "Love of Money."
Why?
Because Interest on Debt grows exponentially, destroying social fabric.
The Jubilee Year (Debt Forgiveness) was the ancient "Reset."

## IV. Strategic Applications

### 4.1 Legacy Planning
Estate Tax.
Dynasty Trusts.

Sustainable goodness.
